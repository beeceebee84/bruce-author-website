About Me

My journey as a writer and storyteller

My Story

I've been captivated by stories for as long as I can remember. As a child, I would spend hours in the local library, devouring books faster than my parents could check them out. By the time I was ten, I had filled numerous notebooks with my own stories—some barely a page long, others spanning entire "novels" (by elementary school standards).

My formal journey into writing began during my undergraduate years, where I studied literature and creative writing. After graduation, I worked various jobs—bookseller, editorial assistant, high school teacher—all while writing in the early mornings and late nights. My first novel was rejected by every publisher I sent it to, but that experience taught me perseverance and the importance of continually honing my craft.

My breakthrough came with the publication of my third novel, which became a surprise bestseller. Since then, I've had the privilege of writing full-time, sharing stories that (I hope) resonate with readers as deeply as my favorite books have resonated with me.

Writing Process

Each book begins differently—sometimes with a character who won't leave my thoughts, sometimes with a historical event that fascinates me, sometimes with a "what if" question that demands exploration. I spend months researching before I begin writing, immersing myself in the world of the story.

My writing routine is sacred. I write every morning, usually from 5:30 AM until noon, in a small studio behind my house. The first draft is always messy—I give myself permission to write badly, knowing I can fix it later. Revision is where the real magic happens, sometimes taking twice as long as the initial draft.

Beyond Writing

When I'm not writing, you'll find me hiking in the mountains near my home, baking bread (badly but enthusiastically), or adding to my collection of vintage typewriters. I'm passionate about literacy initiatives and volunteer regularly with organizations that promote reading and writing education.

I live in the Pacific Northwest with my partner and two extremely spoiled rescue dogs, who often make appearances on my social media accounts.

My Writing Journey

Key moments in my career as an author

First Publication

2008

My short story "Whispered Words" was published in a literary magazine after numerous rejections. This small victory kept me going during the years of unpublished work that followed.

Literary Award

2012

Received the Emerging Writer Award from the National Book Foundation for my debut novel manuscript. Though the novel itself wasn't published, this recognition opened doors to my first literary agent.

Debut Novel

2015

After seven years of writing and revising, my first novel "The Silent Witness" was published. It received critical acclaim and established my reputation as a serious literary voice.

Bestseller Status

2018

"Echoes in the Mist" spent 12 weeks on the New York Times Bestseller list. This commercial success allowed me to transition to writing full-time and establish my current writing routine.

Teaching Position

2021

Began teaching creative writing at the university level, combining my love of writing with my passion for mentoring emerging authors. This experience has profoundly influenced my own work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Inspiration comes from everywhere—historical events, overheard conversations, landscapes that evoke strong emotions. I keep a notebook (actually several) where I jot down anything that sparks my imagination: a newspaper article, an unusual name, an interesting location. These fragments often combine in unexpected ways to form the seeds of stories.

Three pieces of advice: First, read voraciously—both within and outside your preferred genre. Second, write regularly, even when you don't feel inspired. Discipline is more important than talent. Third, don't rush to publish—take time to hone your craft and develop your unique voice. The publishing world will still be there when you're ready.
