Burn Me Whole

(124 reviews)

Bruce did everything right. He survived. But after years of just trying to hold on, he’s left staring at wreckage—an ended marriage, a vanished career, and a man in the mirror he doesn't fucking know. With nothing but a heavy heart, he leaves for Jamaica, not for a vacation, but for a desperate, brutal reckonin. On the island, every silence he swallowed and every armor he wore rises to the surface, demanding he confront the man he’s been and decide who the hell he’s still going to become.

Burn Me Whole is a work of Black Literary Fiction that is raw, unfiltered, and damn honest about the nonlinear journey toward wholeness.This is not a book for surface readers. If you know what it’s like to hide your truths behind armor, this story will hit you where it fucking counts. It doesn't offer easy answers. Instead, it brings the heat—fucking passion, desire, tension—pulling you into the uncomfortable spaces where real healing happens.
